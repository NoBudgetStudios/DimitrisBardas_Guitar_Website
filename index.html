Dimitris Bardas:

Dimitris Bardas is born in Trikala, Greece. He started classical guitar at the age of 8 and he has won numerous prizes in Classical Guitar Competitions hosted in Greece.

In 2011, he took the 7th place at the International Guitar Festival of Belgrade. He obtained his diploma in Classical Guitar in 2015 and at the age of 14, he started experimenting with flamenco.

Ever since his style of playing is flamenco-oriented. He has attended several seminars with recognised guitar players, such as: Javier Conde and Goran Krivokapić. He has also been invited in several Guitar Festivals where he has played as a duet with his former teacher and solist Spyros Tsiolis.

The journey so far:

Dimitris Bardas, a soul born amidst the captivating landscapes of Trikala, Greece, embarked on a profound journey into the realm of classical guitar at the tender age of 8. Within the delicate strings, he discovered not just notes, but the echoes of his own emotions, weaving a tapestry that would resonate with the depths of his being.

His musical odyssey reached remarkable heights as he triumphed in countless Classical Guitar Competitions nestled in the heart of Greece, each victory etching tales of passion into the very chords he strummed. In 2011, the melody of his dedication resonated beyond borders, as he claimed the 7th place at the prestigious International Guitar Festival of Belgrade—a milestone that echoed the harmonies of his unwavering commitment.

At the tender age of 14, Dimitris delved into the enchanting world of flamenco, a genre that became not just a style but the essence of his musical identity. With each impassioned strum, he painted the air with the fiery spirit of flamenco, infusing his notes with the raw emotions that danced within him.

In 2015, he earned his diploma in Classical Guitar, a testament to the years of diligence and fervor poured into mastering his craft. Yet, Dimitris's artistic evolution did not pause; instead, it evolved into a fervent exploration of flamenco, becoming the heartbeat of his musical expression.

His journey became a melodic pilgrimage as he immersed himself in enlightening seminars led by acclaimed guitar virtuosos like Javier Conde and Goran Krivokapić. These encounters became more than mere lessons—they were the forging of connections between souls who spoke the universal language of music.

Dimitris's talent and passion were not confined to solitary performances; he graced numerous Guitar Festivals, sharing the stage in duets with his mentor and maestro, Spyros Tsiolis. The strings of their guitars resonated in harmony, weaving a narrative that transcended the boundaries of sound and emotion, leaving an indelible mark on the hearts of those fortunate enough to bear witness.

In the hands of Dimitris Bardas, the guitar ceased to be just an instrument; it became a vessel for the symphony of his emotions, a conduit through which the spirit of Trikala found its voice in the world. Each strum, each chord, and each note echoed not just his skill but the very essence of his soul, creating a melody that whispered tales of love, longing, and the profound beauty found in the language of music.
